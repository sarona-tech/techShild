FAQs
01
Why are you doing this?
Israel has one of the most advanced technology and innovation hubs in the entire world, it is our duty to ensure its continuation by guarding incredible Israeli startups who’s technology may change industries from the ground up, yet the Iron-Swords Operation in Israel has unjustly impacted their funding, growth or ongoing business functionality.
02
How are companies impacted?
•
Founders, managers or staff called on reserves duty
•
Slower business operation with remaining staff focusing on critical maintenance
•
Difficulty to secure funding, some fundraising processes that started recently are suddenly halted or significantly slowed
03
How does it work?
TechShield will launch a series of reverse-funds with pre-known portfolio companies. Selecting a set of 5-10 companies at a time, who meet the investment criteria, reverse funding them together in a dedicated investment vehicle (a TechShield reverse fund), otherwise known as SPV, and open the vehicle up to Israel-supporting investors from all over the world.
04
Why do you group companies together?
By grouping companies together we manage to support more Israeli companies as well as provide higher diversification and lower risk to investors.
05
Which companies are you targeting?
Companies with a customer base of at least 3 customers, that must be serviced to and maintained, that the current war has directly affected their regular business operation; and are in need of sufficient funding and runway, to ensure the continuation of the business.
06
Who is eligible for investment?
•
Impacted by the war
•
Previously raised Seed or Series-A
•
4-9 months Runway
•
Monthly burn up to $500k
•
Need to maintain service to 3 or more active customers
•
Need to extend runway to 12-18 months
07
Who is behind?
TechShield was initiated by Sarona Ventures, a global VC fund and tech ecosystem investing globally, with deep roots in the Startup Nation. Sarona is not the only VC fund backing this initiative, it is joined by five Israeli and international VC funds who are committed to kick off the funding provided and guarantee the highest level of company selection. TechShield is relying on a technology, fintech and compliance partnership with Fundit, a leading alternative financing platform who is providing its licenced technology to support secure, rapid and streamlined funds collection from Israel-supporting investors from around the world.
08
Who can invest in the reverse funds?
•
Israel supporters from around the world
•
Individuals, institutionals or semi-institutionals investors (family offices, angel investors, trusts, corporations, institutions etc.)
•
Funds, VC funds and Funds of Funds
09
What's the benefit for investors?
•
Safeguard Israel’s economy and its ensure continuation as tech haven and source of innovation and inspiration for the entire world.
•
Hedge your investment by knowing the investment targets before committing an investment, and diversify your participation across several or all TechShield reverse funds.
10
Terms of the investment for Startups?
Multiple of 10 on your 2023 revenues or extention of your most recent valuation/CAP, the higher of the two.
11
Terms of the reverse funds
Each reverse-fund is set for 5 years, with three 1-year extensions; 1% annual (covering direct costs only, zero salaries) and 15% carried interest
12
Why do you limit to companies with a customer base?
•
When you have customers you can lose them.
•
When you have customers you have liability to your customers and shortage of workforce is mission critical affecting actual operations of others.
•
The customers of all Israeli startups are currently, willingly or unwillingly, a part of this war, maintaining high level of service to the global customers of our Israeli companies is a strong, uncompromised statement of stability, sturdiness, resilience and trustworthiness.
•
Early revenue companies are where Sarona’s value proposition is highest and our ability to move the needle and change the course of these companies is immense.
•
There are already several emergency solutions launched to support early stage pre-launch companies, our aim is to support where support is needed
13
Is Sarona and the endorsing funds also investing in these startups?
Yes. Sarona will invest in every TechShield reverse fund out of its own capital. Each of the endorsing funds will also invest their own capital in eligible companies.
14
What's the minimum commitment required from an investor?
No commitment is required in advance, an investor may choose to invest in only one TechShield reverse fund or divide its participation between several or all reverse funds with a minimum investment per reverse fund of US$25,000.
15
How many reverse funds with 5-10 companies you plan to launch?
We aim to support Israeli companies and the Israeli economy, we will continue to launch reverse funds of 5-10 new companies at a time for up to 50 companies, or until we see that the situation has been bettered and the continuation of Israel as the StartupNation has been safeguarded.
16
What is the impact of my investment, how is it making any difference?
By securing sufficient funding to stressed Israeli companies, your capital has direct impact on the continuity and growth of the companies you’ve backed, and imminent impact on the perseverance of the Israeli economy:
•
The salaries of people, that grace to you, will not lose their jobs, and maintain their role in fueling the economy.
•
The export of goods and services the company will provide and bring additional money into the country, as more jobs and as taxes so needed to support the rebuild of the country
